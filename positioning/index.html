Positioning

Positioning is a way to control the layout of elements on a page. There are three types of positioning: static, relative, and absolute. Static positioning is the default positioning of elements. Relative positioning is positioning relative to the normal position of the element. Absolute positioning is positioning relative to the nearest positioned ancestor.


Static

Static positioning is the default positioning of elements. Static positioning is not affected by the top, bottom, left, and right properties.

Relative

Relative positioning is positioning relative to the normal position of the element. Relative positioning is affected by the top, bottom, left, and right properties.


Absolute

Absolute positioning is positioning relative to the nearest positioned parent element. Absolute positioning is affected by the top, bottom, left, and right properties.

Fixed

Fixed positioning is positioning relative to the browser window. Fixed positioning is affected by the top, bottom, left, and right properties.

It stays in place and moves down or up as you scroll.
